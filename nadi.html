Kytie Htee Yoe


Kyite Htee Yoe, also known as the Golden Rock, is a revered pilgrimage site and tourist destination in Myanmar (Burma). Located in the Mon State, it is situated near the town of Kyaikto, approximately 160 kilometers (about 100 miles) east of Yangon, the country's largest city. It is one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Myanmar and is believed to enshrine a hair relic of Gautama Buddha, making it a significant pilgrimage destination for Buddhists. The site is especially popular during the annual pilgrimage season, which usually falls during the Burmese lunar month of Tabaung (usually February or March).
